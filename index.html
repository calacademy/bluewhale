Blue Whales: Past, Present, Future

  • Two centuries ago, an estimated 200,000 blue whales swam the world’s oceans.

  • Hunting with harpoon cannons, invented in 1890, drove blue whales to near extinction.

  • Since the 1970s, hunting bans and endangered species protection helped populations increase.

  • Today, an estimated 10,000 – 25,000 blue whales swim the world’s oceans.

  • In today’s busy coastal waters, ship strikes, fishing gear, and underwater noise can injure or kill whales.

  • Changes to water temperature and acidity a ect blue whales’ only food source.

  • Shifts in amounts and locations of krill may drive changes in blue whale migration.

  • How well will blue whales adapt to climate change? Many questions leave their future uncertain.

A Moveable Feast

Tap a Spot to Explore

Appetite

Earth’s largest animal relies on a diet of tiny shrimp-like krill. A blue whale can devour 500 kg (1,100 lbs) of krill in a single mouthful. That’s half a million calories—it’s like eating 2,500 tacos in one bite.

Mouth

A blue whale feeds by lunging, opening its mouth, allowing its pleated throat to expand, and scooping up krill and water. It uses the bristle-like baleen lining its top jaw to strain krill from each mouthful of water.

Blowhole

A whale’s blowhole is like human nostrils. A single, krill-scented exhale can send spray shooting 3 stories (9 m / 30 ft) into the air. The sound of a blow can be heard from miles away.

Heart

At rest, a blue whale’s heart beats only about 6 times per minute. Its active heart rate (about 30 bpm) is slower than a human’s resting heart rate. The sound of its car-sized heart can be detected from 3 km (2 miles) away.

Size

Blue whales are the largest animals that ever lived, weighing as much as 200 metric tons and growing to 30 m (100 ft) in length — bigger than the dinosaurs. That’s the length of 3 school buses.

Speed

Powerful tail flukes propel blue whales at up to 48 km/h (30 mph). That’s 6 times faster than the swiftest human swimmer. Opening its mouth to gulp krill slows a whale. It won’t bother unless the quantity of krill is worth the energy.
